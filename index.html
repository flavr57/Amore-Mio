GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications, with investment fund routes and rehabilitation projects in low-density areas remaining the most accessible pathways. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is also thriving, requiring proof of remote income of at least four times Portugal's minimum wage — a straightforward bar for most remote workers relocating from the US.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen sustained upward pressure on everyday costs, with groceries and dining out running roughly 30–40% below California levels — still a favorable gap, though narrowing year over year. The Algarve and smaller interior towns remain meaningfully more affordable and are increasingly popular among expats seeking the Portuguese lifestyle without the capital-city price tag.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the old NHR scheme — the IFICI incentive, also called NHR 2.0 — offers qualifying new residents a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-source income and potential exemptions on foreign-source income for up to ten years. US citizens should still plan carefully around FATCA obligations, but for many remote workers and retirees, the regime remains one of Europe's most generous tax welcomes.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon have plateaued somewhat following government intervention and rent-control measures, but availability in desirable central neighborhoods is still tight. Buyers in Porto and the Silver Coast are finding better value, with property prices per square meter running well below equivalent coastal areas in France or Spain — though demand from international buyers keeps upward pressure alive.

Red Light Therapy for Bees Could Help Struggling Pollinator Populations
Scientists are exploring whether red light therapy — long used in human medicine to promote healing and reduce inflammation — might also benefit bee colonies under stress, offering a novel tool in the fight to protect pollinators. Early findings suggest exposure to specific wavelengths of red light may improve bee vitality and resilience, a quietly radical idea at the intersection of photobiology and ecological conservation. Given that roughly a third of our global food supply depends on pollinators, this bright little idea carries serious weight.

Spring Is the Perfect Moment to Divide Perennials Before Summer Heat
If you have perennials or ornamental grasses that have been quietly crowding themselves out, this weekend is genuinely one of the last good windows to divide them before the season turns warm and the work becomes stressful for the plants. Dividing not only rejuvenates tired clumps and improves flowering, but gives you new plants to tuck into other spots — or gift to a neighbor. Dig generously, keep the roots moist, and replant at the same depth; they'll reward you handsomely by midsummer.
