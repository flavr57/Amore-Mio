GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications through qualifying investment funds, real estate in interior regions, and cultural contributions — direct urban property purchases in Lisbon and Porto remain excluded. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa remains an attractive parallel route, requiring proof of remote income of roughly €3,480/month for 2026.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto continue to see modest but steady inflation in grocery and dining costs, though both cities remain meaningfully cheaper than comparable Western European capitals for daily living. The Algarve and Silver Coast offer noticeably lower costs for those willing to step outside the major urban centers.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the original NHR — the IFICI regime (also called NHR 2.0) — offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-source income and exemptions on many foreign-source income streams for qualifying new residents for up to ten years. Expats with remote income or pensions should consult a Portuguese tax advisor to confirm eligibility before applying for residency.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Property prices in Lisbon have stabilized somewhat after years of sharp rises, but rental inventory in prime neighborhoods remains tight and competitive heading into summer 2026. Those open to Silver Coast towns like Caldas da Rainha or interior Alentejo villages will find both better value and a warmer welcome from local communities.
