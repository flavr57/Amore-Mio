Lisbon and Porto continue to see upward pressure on everyday costs, with grocery and dining prices rising modestly in line with broader European inflation trends. NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the original NHR scheme — the IFICI regime, also called NHR 2.0 — offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and exemptions on most foreign income for qualifying new residents over a 10-year period. Expats with skills in technology, research, or the arts are among those who qualify, making it one of Europe's most attractive fiscal incentives for newcomers.

Rental prices in Lisbon and Porto remain elevated, with central one-bedroom apartments averaging €1,400–€1,800 per month, though supply is slowly improving as new construction comes online. Scientists Map Butternut Tree Survivors to Guide a Forest's Comeback
The butternut tree — a graceful relative of the black walnut, beloved for its pale wood and the wildlife it sustains — has been devastated by a fungal disease for decades, pushing it toward the edge of disappearance in North America. Now, scientists have carefully tracked the locations of trees that have naturally developed resistance to the disease, creating a roadmap for targeted reforestation efforts that work with nature rather than against it. It's conservation at its most hopeful: listening to the forest's own survivors to bring it back.

Red Light Therapy for Bees? A Bright Idea to Help Struggling Pollinators
Red light therapy — long used by humans to improve skin health, ease muscle soreness, and slow aging — is now being explored as a potential tool to support bee populations facing environmental stress and colony decline. Early research suggests that targeted wavelengths of red light may boost bee vitality and resilience, offering a gentle, non-chemical way to support the pollinators that underpin so much of our food supply. Sometimes the most imaginative solutions come from asking a simple, beautiful question: what if something that helps us might also help them?

Spring Is the Last Good Moment to Divide Your Perennials and Grasses
If you've been meaning to divide overgrown perennials or ornamental grasses, this weekend is your window — once the heat of summer settles in, the stress on divided roots becomes too great, and the next opportunity won't come until autumn. Dig clumps cleanly, separate into healthy sections with a sharp spade, replant at the same depth, and water generously to help new roots establish. It's one of the most rewarding acts in gardening: one plant becoming many, the garden quietly multiplying itself.
