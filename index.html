COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen continued upward pressure on everyday expenses, with groceries and dining out noticeably pricier than a few years ago — though still meaningfully more affordable than coastal California. NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the old NHR scheme — the IFICI regime, sometimes called NHR 2.0 — grants qualifying new residents a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced professional income and potential exemptions on certain foreign income for up to ten years. It remains one of Europe's more generous tax incentive programs for new arrivals from non-EU countries.

Rental prices in Lisbon and Porto have stabilized somewhat following government intervention, but availability of long-term rentals in desirable neighborhoods remains tight. Dutch Women Surge Into Local Politics With Clever Preferential Vote Campaign
In the Netherlands, a grassroots 'Vote for a Woman' campaign helped propel 504 women into seats in recent local elections by encouraging voters to use their preferential votes strategically. It's a quietly radical idea: that representation can shift not only through legislation but through collective, informed choice.

Can Red Light Therapy Help Save the World's Struggling Bee Populations?
Scientists are exploring whether red light therapy — long used in human medicine to improve skin health and reduce inflammation — might also benefit bees, whose populations have been declining at alarming rates due to pesticides, habitat loss, and disease. Early research suggests that targeted wavelengths of red light could boost bee vitality and resilience in ways that might meaningfully support pollinator recovery. It's the kind of cross-disciplinary leap that reminds us how much human ingenuity, applied with care, can ripple outward into the natural world.

Festival Urine Is Growing an Entirely New Biodiverse Forest in the UK
In a wonderfully unexpected first for the United Kingdom, a new woodland is being planted and nourished using odor-free nutrients recovered from urine collected at music festival toilets. It is, in the best possible way, proof that the circular economy can find poetry in the most unlikely places.

A Dog's Devotion: The Science Behind Why Dogs Choose to Stay Close
Research in animal cognition continues to confirm what every dog owner already knows in their bones: dogs don't merely tolerate our company — they actively seek it as a source of comfort and security, releasing oxytocin when they make eye contact with their humans in a feedback loop nearly identical to parent-child bonding. Studies from Azabu University in Japan showed that simply gazing at your dog raises both your oxytocin levels and theirs, a mutual emotional loop no other domesticated species has developed with humans. On a quiet Sunday morning, that slow blink from across the room is not nothing — it is, by every biological measure, love.
