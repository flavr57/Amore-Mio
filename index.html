Portugal Watch
✓
GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to attract international applicants, with investment funds and real estate in low-density areas remaining eligible pathways. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is also thriving, offering a clear route for remote workers earning foreign income — ideal if you're planning to work from a Lisbon café terrace.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen steady price increases in dining, groceries, and services as expat demand remains strong, though both cities still offer notable savings compared to major US coastal cities. The Algarve and interior regions remain considerably more affordable for those willing to explore beyond the capital.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the old NHR scheme — the IFICI regime, also called NHR 2.0 — offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-source income for eligible new residents, plus exemptions on most foreign-source income for a 10-year period. Qualifying professions include tech, scientific research, and highly skilled roles, making it well worth a conversation with a Portuguese tax adviser before your move.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon remain elevated, with central neighborhoods like Príncipe Real and Chiado commanding premium rates, while areas like Mouraria and Beato offer better value with rising charm. Buying property has become competitive, but those moving from California coastal markets will still find the prices — and the ocean views — a welcome shift.

Why Your Orchid Drops Blooms — and How to Stop It
Phalaenopsis orchids are famously dramatic about their environment: sudden temperature shifts, drafts from air conditioning, or even ethylene gas from ripening fruit nearby can trigger rapid bloom drop. The fix is simpler than you'd think — find a stable spot away from vents and fruit bowls, water with room-temperature water every 10–14 days, and let the roots dry slightly between waterings. As you prepare for a move to Portugal's gentle Atlantic climate, know that your orchid will likely thrive there — Lisbon's humidity and filtered light are genuinely orchid-friendly.
