GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications in 2026, with investment fund routes and approved scientific research projects among the eligible pathways. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa remains an attractive parallel option for remote workers earning above the threshold, with processing times stabilizing in most consulates.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto continue to see above-average inflation in dining, services, and everyday goods compared to just a few years ago, though both cities remain meaningfully more affordable than comparable Western European capitals. The Algarve and smaller inland towns still offer excellent value for newcomers willing to look beyond the most popular neighborhoods.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's successor to the original NHR — the IFICI regime — offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and exemptions on most foreign-sourced income for qualifying new residents for a period of ten years. Applicants who establish tax residency in Portugal and have not been residents in the prior five years remain eligible to apply.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Property prices in Lisbon and the Algarve remain elevated, with demand from international buyers keeping the market competitive even as mortgage rates soften slightly. Renting before buying is still widely advised for newcomers — it allows time to learn the neighborhoods while the market finds its footing.

As temperatures warm and days lengthen, bougainvillea enters its most enthusiastic season — and a little strategic pruning right now will reward you with an explosion of vivid color well into summer.
