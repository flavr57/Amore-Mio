GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues under its investment fund and cultural donation routes, with real estate purchases largely excluded since 2023 — making fund-based applications the cleaner path for 2026 applicants. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa remains one of Europe's most accessible remote-work visas, requiring proof of roughly €3,280/month in income for a one-year renewable stay.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen sustained upward pressure on grocery, dining, and service costs over the past two years, though both cities remain meaningfully more affordable than comparable Western European capitals. The Algarve offers a gentler cost curve for day-to-day life, though seasonal tourism inflates prices between May and September.

NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the original NHR — the IFICI regime, sometimes called NHR 2.0 — offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-source income for qualifying new residents for up to ten years. Foreign pension and passive income treatment has shifted, so a conversation with a Portuguese tax advisor before arrival is genuinely worthwhile.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon and Porto remain elevated, with central one-bedroom apartments typically ranging from €1,200 to €1,800/month, though inland and coastal towns offer considerably better value. The government's ongoing efforts to cool the market through rent controls and incentives for long-term landlords are worth watching as policies continue to evolve.
