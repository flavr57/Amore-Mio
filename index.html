GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues under its restructured 2024 framework, with investment funds and artistic production among the qualifying routes — real estate in Lisbon and Porto remains excluded, keeping the program alive for those willing to invest creatively. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa remains one of Europe's most welcoming paths for remote workers, requiring proof of roughly €3,280/month in income.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen continued upward pressure on everyday costs, with groceries and dining out running noticeably higher than just two years ago — though still meaningfully below comparable coastal cities in France or Spain. The Algarve and smaller interior towns remain a genuine bargain, offering a slower, sunnier life at a fraction of what California demands.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the old NHR scheme — the IFICI regime, sometimes called NHR 2.0 — offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income for qualifying new residents, plus potential exemptions on most foreign income for a ten-year period. It's particularly attractive for retirees with foreign pensions and remote workers earning abroad, and applications can be filed once you establish tax residency.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon have stabilized somewhat after years of sharp increases, but availability of long-term rentals remains tight and landlords often favor short-term tourist lets. Buying property is increasingly popular among expats as a hedge — prices are still appreciating in key urban areas, so moving quickly once you arrive tends to reward decisiveness.

Brazil Now Grants Pets Legal Custody Rights After Couples Separate
Brazil's Congress has passed a landmark law allowing courts to grant joint custody of pets to separated couples — a legal recognition that animals are emotional family members, not mere property to be divided. Lawmakers framed the change as a reflection of the deep bonds modern families form with their animals, and the legislation provides a framework for custody arrangements, visits, and shared financial responsibility. It places Brazil among a growing number of countries rethinking the legal status of companion animals.

Egypt — one of the sunniest and windiest countries on Earth — has announced a sweeping new renewables project that will generate enough clean energy to power six million homes, part of a broader regional response to the current oil price shock rippling out from the Iran conflict. Energy analysts see it as a signal that geopolitical instability in fossil fuel markets is accelerating the clean energy shift faster than policy ever did.

Small Trees That Thrive in Pots — Perfect for Your Future Portuguese Terrace
Japanese maples and hardy palms top the list of small trees that deliver maximum beauty in compact spaces — and both translate beautifully to the terrace gardens so common in Lisbon and Algarve villas. A Japanese maple in a generous pot needs bright indirect light, consistent moisture, and a sheltered spot away from harsh afternoon sun, rewarding you with fiery seasonal color. Hardy palms, meanwhile, practically thrive on neglect once established, and their silhouette against a whitewashed wall is pure Portuguese magic.
