GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program remains open in 2026, with investment fund routes continuing to attract international applicants — ideal for those not purchasing residential real estate. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is also thriving, with streamlined processing making it one of Europe's most accessible remote-worker pathways.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto continue to see gentle upward pressure on everyday costs, with dining and groceries running roughly 20–30% below comparable California expenses — but the gap is narrowing. The Algarve and interior regions remain meaningfully more affordable, rewarding those willing to explore beyond the capital.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the original NHR — the IFICI regime — offers a flat 20% income tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and potential exemptions on foreign income for qualifying new residents for a 10-year period. American retirees and remote workers with non-Portuguese income sources continue to find it one of Europe's most attractive fiscal frameworks.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon and Porto remain elevated by historical standards, with desirable one-bedroom apartments in central neighborhoods ranging from €1,200 to €1,800 per month. Buyers are finding slightly more negotiating room than in prior years, but demand from international relocators continues to keep the market competitive.
