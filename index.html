GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications in 2026, with fund investments and rehabilitation projects in low-density areas remaining the most viable routes. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is also actively welcoming remote workers earning above the income threshold, making Portugal one of Europe's most accessible destinations for relocation.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen notable price increases in dining, groceries, and services over the past two years, though both cities remain meaningfully more affordable than comparable coastal California living. The Algarve and interior regions like the Alentejo still offer exceptional value and are increasingly popular with relocating Americans.

NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's updated IFICI regime — the successor to the original NHR — continues to offer a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and favorable treatment for qualifying foreign-sourced income for a ten-year period. New residents who apply promptly after registering their tax number can still benefit from this significant advantage.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Property prices in Lisbon and the Algarve remain elevated by historical standards, with rental inventory tight in central neighborhoods — patience and flexibility on location pay dividends. Buyers and renters willing to explore emerging neighborhoods or smaller cities like Setúbal or Évora are finding considerably better value in 2026.

Chernobyl at 40: What Pripyat Looks Like Four Decades On
This week marks forty years since the catastrophic explosion at the Chernobyl nuclear plant forced the overnight evacuation of Pripyat — a city of 50,000 people who left behind everything, never to return. Today, nature has quietly reclaimed much of what humans abandoned: forests push through apartment floors, wildlife roams streets once filled with Soviet-era families. It is one of history's most haunting accidental rewilding experiments, and a profound meditation on resilience, impermanence, and the long memory of the earth.
