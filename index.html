Fourteen nations gathered at the Panathenaic Stadium, reviving one of humanity's oldest celebrations of excellence and unity. It's a reminder that even the most beloved traditions can be lost, and then beautifully found again.

GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues under its current investment-based framework, with real estate routes limited to specific interior and low-density areas. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa remains a popular pathway for remote workers, requiring proof of income roughly four times Portugal's minimum wage.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto continue to see gradual price increases in dining, services, and everyday goods, though both cities remain notably more affordable than most Western European capitals. The Algarve offers a somewhat gentler cost curve outside peak tourist season, making it worth exploring as a base.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for NHR — the IFICI regime (also called NHR 2.0) — offers a flat 20% income tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income for qualifying new residents, including certain professionals and retirees. Applying in your first year of tax residency is essential, so early planning with a local fiscal adviser is strongly recommended.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon remain elevated by historical standards, with demand outpacing supply in central neighborhoods; Porto and smaller towns offer better value for long-term renters. Property purchase prices have plateaued slightly but have not meaningfully corrected, so renting first to explore regions remains a wise strategy.

More than 100 people — the majority of them children — have died from suspected measles in Bangladesh since mid-March, prompting authorities to launch an emergency vaccination campaign. Global health advocates are using the crisis to renew calls for sustained investment in childhood immunization infrastructure across South Asia.

Scotland's largest greenhouse, a grand Victorian glasshouse in Glasgow that has sat derelict for forty years, is finally being saved — with $1.5 million secured to transform it into a 'living ruin' and new event space. It's a gorgeous reminder that preservation doesn't always mean perfection — sometimes it means letting a place breathe again.
