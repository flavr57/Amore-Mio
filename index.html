GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to attract international investors in 2026, with fund-based and rehabilitation investment routes remaining the primary qualifying pathways since direct real estate purchases were removed. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is also thriving, offering a clear route for remote workers earning above the monthly income threshold to establish legal residency.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen sustained upward pressure on everyday expenses, with dining, groceries, and utilities notably higher than they were just three years ago — though still meaningfully below comparable Western European capitals. The Algarve and smaller interior towns remain attractively priced for those willing to trade urban buzz for a gentler pace.

NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the original NHR — now called IFICI (the Incentivo Fiscal à Investigação Científica e Inovação) — offers a flat 20% income tax rate for qualifying new residents, including certain professionals, researchers, and entrepreneurs, for up to ten years. Those who secured NHR status before the 2024 cutoff remain grandfathered under the original favorable terms.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Portugal's rental market remains competitive in major cities, with Lisbon one-bedroom apartments averaging €1,400–€1,800 per month in central neighborhoods, though outer districts and smaller cities offer considerably better value. Property purchase prices have plateaued somewhat from their recent peaks, giving buyers a bit more breathing room to negotiate in early 2026.

A 3,500-Year-Old Loom Rewrites What We Know About Bronze Age Weaving
A wooden loom miraculously preserved by fire some 3,500 years ago has yielded remarkable new insights into the sophistication of Bronze Age textile production — a revolution in craft that shaped trade, identity, and culture across the ancient world. Most of the loom weights survived intact, allowing researchers to reconstruct weaving techniques with unusual precision. It's a gorgeous reminder that destruction sometimes preserves what time would otherwise erase.
