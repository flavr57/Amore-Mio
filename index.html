GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues under its updated framework, with fund investments and rehabilitation projects remaining the primary qualifying routes. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is fully operational and remains one of Europe's most welcoming pathways for remote workers earning foreign income.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto continue to see upward pressure on everyday costs, with groceries and dining out running roughly 20–30% higher than just three years ago, though still well below comparable coastal California prices. The Algarve and Silver Coast remain comparatively affordable for those willing to live slightly outside the tourist corridors.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the original NHR — the IFICI regime (also called NHR 2.0) — offers qualifying new residents a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and significant exemptions on most foreign income for a 10-year period. Applications are processed through the Portuguese Tax Authority and are best initiated alongside your residency registration.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Property prices in Lisbon remain elevated, with average per-square-meter costs in prime neighborhoods exceeding €5,000, and rental vacancy in the city center staying historically tight. Buyers and renters finding more value are increasingly looking to Setúbal, Braga, and the western Algarve, where quality of life is high and prices have not yet fully caught up.

As days lengthen and light intensifies through April, your orchids are quietly waking up — this is the ideal moment to begin a diluted balanced fertilizer routine every two weeks and to check whether roots have outgrown their pots.
