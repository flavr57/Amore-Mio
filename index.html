GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications focused on qualifying investment funds, capital transfers, and job-creation ventures — real estate routes remain closed to new applicants. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa remains a popular and accessible alternative for remote workers, requiring proof of monthly income of at least four times Portugal's minimum wage.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto continue to see gradual price increases in everyday goods and dining, though Portugal remains meaningfully more affordable than most Western European capitals. The Algarve and interior regions such as the Alentejo offer a noticeably gentler cost of living for those willing to settle outside the major cities.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the original NHR scheme — the IFICI incentive for new residents — offers a flat 20% income tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and exemptions on many foreign-sourced earnings for a period of ten years. New applicants should register with Portuguese tax authorities within the required window after establishing residency to secure eligibility.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon and Porto remain elevated, with demand consistently outpacing supply in desirable central neighborhoods. Savvy movers are finding better value in the city's outer parishes and in secondary cities like Braga, Setúbal, and Évora, where quality of life is high and competition considerably lower.

EU's Highest Court Grants Online Gamblers Right to Seek Compensation
The European Court of Justice has issued a landmark ruling allowing online gamblers to sue for losses incurred when gambling was illegal in their home country — even if the laws have since changed. The decision marks a significant expansion of consumer protection rights within the EU's digital single market, and could open the door to a wave of claims against offshore gambling operators. Legal scholars see it as part of a broader trend of European courts asserting citizens' rights in the fast-moving digital economy.

Pedestrian Road Deaths in the US Fall at Their Fastest Rate Ever
New data shows that pedestrian traffic fatalities in the United States declined by nearly 11% in the first half of 2025 compared to the same period the previous year — the steepest single-year drop since records began. The improvement is attributed to a combination of improved street design, lower post-pandemic traffic volumes, and expanded pedestrian safety legislation in several states. While overall numbers remain too high, the trend offers genuine cause for optimism about the direction of road safety in America.

Give Your Orchid the April Reset It Has Been Waiting For
April's longer days make it the ideal moment to refresh your orchid's growing conditions — move it to a spot with bright, indirect morning light and check whether the roots have outgrown their pot, since overcrowding is the most common reason for reluctant blooming. If the aerial roots are silvery-white and plump, your watering rhythm is working beautifully; if they look shriveled, increase frequency slightly and consider a diluted orchid fertilizer every other week through June. A little attentive repotting now can reward you with a spectacular second flush of flowers by late summer.

Sweet Peas Need Sowing Now — Don't Let the Window Close
If you've been meaning to sow sweet peas, this week is genuinely your last comfortable chance before the season tips against you — sow them into deep pots or root trainers, as their long roots resent disturbance, and place them somewhere cool and bright. Pinch out the growing tip once two pairs of leaves appear to encourage bushy, flower-laden growth rather than a single lanky stem. In a few weeks you'll have cascading, fragrant blooms that make every morning walk through the garden feel like a small ceremony.

The Loyal Dog Who Waited: A Story of Devotion That Defined a Village
In the small Italian village of Borgo Val di Taro, a golden retriever named Ettore became a local legend after waiting each afternoon at the bus stop where his elderly owner had last departed — a vigil that moved the entire community to quietly look after him. Neighbors took turns feeding him, and the local café left a water bowl at the corner; in time, Ettore became the town's unofficial greeter, welcoming every returning bus with the same unwavering optimism. Some dogs, it seems, teach us more about fidelity and hope than a library full of books ever could.
