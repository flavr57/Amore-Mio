Portugal Watch
✓
GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications in 2026, with investment fund routes and real estate in interior regions remaining the most popular pathways. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is also thriving, requiring proof of remote income of at least four times Portugal's minimum wage — a very achievable bar for most California-based professionals.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen continued upward pressure on day-to-day costs, with groceries and dining out noticeably pricier than three years ago, though still well below major Western European capitals. The Algarve and smaller inland towns remain genuinely affordable, making location choice one of the most important decisions in your Portugal planning.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the old NHR scheme — the IFICI regime, sometimes called NHR 2.0 — offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and favorable treatment of foreign-sourced income for qualifying new residents for up to ten years. Americans relocating and registering as tax residents in 2026 can still apply, making the tax picture for thoughtful movers quite attractive.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Portugal's rental market remains tight in Lisbon and the Algarve, with quality apartments in central neighborhoods commanding premium prices and often moving fast. Buying is increasingly popular among relocating expats as a way to lock in costs, though property prices in sought-after areas have continued their steady climb into 2026.

Bougainvillea's Spring Secret: Why Now Is Its Most Important Moment
As the days lengthen in late March, bougainvillea emerges from its semi-dormant winter rest hungry for attention — this is exactly the right moment to give it a modest but purposeful pruning, cutting back leggy stems by about a third to encourage the dense new growth that will eventually erupt into those spectacular magenta bracts. Follow the pruning with a balanced fertilizer that leans slightly toward phosphorus, which drives flowering rather than leaf production. In Hermosa Beach's mild coastal climate, your bougainvillea is likely already showing new green tips — a lovely sign that the season's first real show is not far off.

Orchid After-Bloom Care: The Ritual That Brings Them Back
When your Phalaenopsis orchid drops its last flower, resist every instinct to set it aside and forget it — this post-bloom window is actually when the plant is most receptive to the care that will determine whether it re-spikes in three to six months. Cut the old spike just above the second or third node from the base (leaving those nodes intact gives the plant a shortcut to a new flower spike), then move the orchid to a spot where it receives bright, indirect light and experiences a gentle ten-degree temperature drop at night, which is the biological trigger for re-blooming. Water thoroughly once a week, let it drain completely, and feed lightly with a balanced orchid fertilizer every other watering — then simply trust the process.

The Science of Why Your Dog Always Knows When You Are Sad
Research published in recent years has confirmed what dog lovers have long suspected: dogs possess a remarkable, cross-species ability to read human emotional states through a sophisticated combination of scent detection, facial expression reading, and vocal tone recognition. Studies show that dogs actually preferentially approach humans who are crying over those who are humming contentedly — suggesting not just awareness, but something that looks very much like empathetic motivation. Whether you call it instinct or affection, the dog curled against you on a hard day is not imagining your need for comfort; they genuinely sense it.
