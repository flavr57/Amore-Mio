GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications through qualifying investment routes, including venture capital funds and cultural donations, following the removal of real estate as an eligible category in 2023. The pathway remains a viable option for non-EU nationals seeking residency with a route to citizenship after five years.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen continued upward pressure on everyday costs, with groceries and dining out running noticeably higher than just two years ago — though both cities remain meaningfully more affordable than comparable Western European capitals. The Algarve and smaller inland towns still offer excellent value, especially for those flexible about location.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the original NHR — the IFICI regime, also called NHR 2.0 — offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-source income and exemptions on most foreign-source income for qualifying new residents for a ten-year period. Applications are filed through the Portuguese Tax Authority (AT) and must be submitted by March 31 of the year following the year you establish residency.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Property prices in Lisbon remain elevated, with prime neighborhoods like Príncipe Real and Chiado commanding €6,000–€8,000+ per square meter, while rental supply in the capital stays tight. Those open to Porto's western suburbs, the Silver Coast, or the Alentejo region will find considerably more breathing room in both purchase and rental markets.
