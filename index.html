Portugal Watch
✓
GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications focused on investment funds, capital transfers, and job creation — real estate investment was removed from eligibility in 2023, but the pathway remains open and active. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is thriving, welcoming remote workers who earn at least four times Portugal's minimum wage from non-Portuguese sources.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen continued upward pressure on everyday costs, with groceries and dining out running roughly 30–40% cheaper than Southern California but noticeably pricier than just a few years ago. The Algarve and smaller interior towns still offer significantly more affordable day-to-day living for those willing to venture beyond the capital.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's replacement for the original NHR scheme — the IFICI regime, sometimes called NHR 2.0 — offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and potential exemptions on certain foreign income for qualifying new residents for up to ten years. Retirees and remote workers with foreign pension or employment income may find the structure particularly advantageous; consulting a Portuguese tax advisor remains essential.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Property prices in Lisbon have stabilized somewhat after years of steep climbs, but rental inventory in desirable central neighborhoods remains tight, pushing many newcomers toward Porto, Setúbal, or the Silver Coast for better value. Buying is increasingly competitive, with international demand keeping prices firm — budgeting €3,000–€5,000/month for a quality Lisbon rental or exploring purchase early in your planning is wise.

North America's Largest Wildlife Overpass Opens, Giving Animals Safe Passage
Moose, elk, black bears, and mountain lions near Larkspur, Colorado now have a dedicated bridge to safely cross six lanes of I-25 — the largest wildlife overpass on the continent, and a milestone in the movement to reconnect fragmented animal habitats. The structure represents years of collaboration between engineers, wildlife biologists, and transportation authorities who believed the highway didn't have to be a death sentence for migrating animals. It's infrastructure with a soul, and a reminder that human progress and ecological stewardship don't have to be opposites.

Cloudy Days Are Actually Perfect for Repotting Your Orchids
Overcast mornings like this one in Hermosa Beach are ideal for orchid repotting — the diffused light means no transplant shock from direct sun, and cooler temperatures let roots settle in gently before a plant has to work hard. If your Phalaenopsis has roots creeping over the pot's edge or the bark medium has broken down to mush, this weekend is your invitation. Choose a clear pot one size up, use fresh orchid bark, and resist the urge to water for four or five days afterward to encourage the roots to reach outward.

Spring Is Bougainvillea's Cue — Here's How to Encourage Blooms
As days grow longer heading into April, bougainvillea wakes up hungry and ambitious — this is the moment to begin a light feeding with a low-nitrogen, high-phosphorus fertilizer to push flowering rather than leafy growth. Bougainvillea actually blooms more abundantly when mildly stressed, so hold back on watering slightly and let the soil dry out between sessions. If you're planning to bring one to Portugal, you'll be delighted to know it thrives magnificently along the Algarve coast — practically the national plant of every whitewashed garden wall.

Research published in recent years has confirmed what every dog owner has quietly always known: dogs are exquisitely attuned to human emotional states, reading micro-expressions, body posture, and even chemical signals in ways that exceed most primates.
