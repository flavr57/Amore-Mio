GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications through qualifying investment routes, including venture capital funds and cultural donations, following the removal of real estate as an eligible category in 2023. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa remains a popular pathway, requiring proof of remote income of at least four times Portugal's minimum wage.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen sustained upward pressure on everyday costs, with dining, groceries, and utilities roughly 20–30% higher than five years ago, though still meaningfully below comparable Western European capitals. The Algarve and interior regions remain noticeably more affordable for those willing to settle outside the major urban centers.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's updated IFICI regime (the successor to NHR) offers new residents qualifying in approved high-value professions a flat 20% income tax rate on Portuguese-source income for ten years. Foreign pension and passive income treatment has shifted, so consulting a licensed Portuguese tax advisor before your move is highly recommended.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Property prices in Lisbon and the Algarve remain elevated, with demand from international buyers keeping competition brisk in desirable neighborhoods. Rental inventory in central Lisbon is still tight, though longer-term leases in quieter districts and smaller cities like Setúbal or Braga offer more breathing room for new arrivals.
