GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to attract international applicants, with investment funds and real estate in low-density areas remaining eligible pathways. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is also active and well-suited for remote workers earning foreign income — a strong option if you're working for a US employer while living in Portugal.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen meaningful price increases over the past few years, with groceries, dining, and services running noticeably higher than even three years ago. The Algarve and smaller interior towns still offer significantly more affordable day-to-day living, making location choice a key financial lever.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's updated IFICI regime (the successor to NHR) offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and potential exemptions on foreign income for qualifying new residents for up to ten years. Applying early in the year of your tax residency registration is essential — speaking with a Portuguese fiscal attorney before you move is highly recommended.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon have stabilized somewhat following government intervention, but demand still outpaces supply in desirable neighborhoods like Príncipe Real and Alfama. Buyers are finding slightly more negotiating room in 2026 compared to the 2023–2024 peak, though quality properties in coastal areas remain competitive.
