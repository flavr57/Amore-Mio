GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications focused on investment funds, cultural contributions, and job creation — with real estate purchases in high-density areas still excluded. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa remains a popular and accessible pathway for remote workers, requiring proof of income at roughly four times Portugal's minimum wage.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen steady price increases in dining, services, and everyday groceries, though costs remain meaningfully lower than comparable European capitals like Paris or Amsterdam. The Algarve and interior regions still offer excellent value, particularly for those willing to explore beyond the most tourist-dense areas.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's updated IFICI regime — the successor to the original NHR — offers a flat 20% income tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income for qualifying new residents, with favorable treatment for certain foreign-source income streams. Applying early in your first year of tax residency is essential, so consulting a Portuguese fiscal advisor well before your move is strongly recommended.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon and Porto remain elevated by historical standards, with demand outpacing supply in desirable central neighborhoods — expect to budget €1,200–€2,000/month for a quality one-bedroom in either city. Silver linings exist in secondary cities like Braga, Setúbal, and the Silver Coast, where more spacious properties are available at significantly gentler prices.
