GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications focused on investment funds, cultural heritage donations, and job creation — real estate no longer qualifies in most areas, making fund-based routes the most popular path for Americans relocating from California. Processing times have improved in 2026, with many applicants reporting approvals within 8–12 months.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen continued upward pressure on everyday costs, with dining, groceries, and services nudging closer to Western European norms — though both cities remain meaningfully more affordable than Los Angeles or the Bay Area. The Algarve and smaller inland towns still offer genuinely lower costs, making location choice within Portugal increasingly important.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's updated IFICI regime (the successor to NHR) remains attractive for new residents, offering a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and favorable treatment of certain foreign income for a 10-year period. Americans should note that US citizens must still file US taxes, but the IFICI benefit can meaningfully reduce the overall tax burden when structured carefully with a qualified advisor.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon's most desirable neighborhoods — Príncipe Real, Chiado, and Alfama — have stabilized somewhat in early 2026 but remain elevated compared to three years ago, with quality two-bedroom apartments typically ranging from €1,800–€2,800/month. Buyers are finding slightly more negotiating room in the property purchase market as higher interest rates have cooled some competition.
