GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to accept applications in 2026, with investment fund routes and rehabilitation property options remaining the most popular pathways. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is also thriving, requiring proof of remote income above roughly four times Portugal's minimum wage — very manageable for most US-based remote workers.
~
COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen noticeable price increases over the past two years, with dining, groceries, and services creeping upward — though both cities remain significantly more affordable than comparable US coastal metros. The Algarve and Silver Coast offer gentler price points if flexibility on location is possible.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's updated IFICI regime (successor to the original NHR) offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and potential exemptions on certain foreign income for qualifying new residents over a 10-year period. It's worth engaging a Portuguese tax advisor early, as the application must be submitted in the year you establish residency.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental demand in Lisbon and Porto remains high, keeping prices elevated — a one-bedroom in central Lisbon typically runs €1,400–€1,900/month in 2026. Buying continues to offer better long-term value, though inventory in prime neighborhoods is tight and competition from international buyers remains steady.
