GOLDEN VISA
Portugal's Golden Visa program continues to attract international applicants in 2026, with investment fund routes remaining the most popular pathway following the removal of direct real estate purchases. The D8 Digital Nomad Visa also remains active and welcoming, requiring proof of remote income above roughly four times Portugal's minimum wage.

COST OF LIVING
Lisbon and Porto have seen living costs rise noticeably over the past two years, with dining, groceries, and utilities creeping closer to Western European norms — though they still offer meaningful savings compared to Southern California. Smaller cities like Setúbal, Braga, or the Silver Coast remain genuinely affordable and worth serious consideration.
✓
NHR TAX REGIME
Portugal's updated IFICI regime (successor to the original NHR program) offers a flat 20% tax rate on Portuguese-sourced income and favorable treatment of certain foreign income for qualifying new residents for up to ten years. Expats who register before December 31, 2026 may still access the transitional provisions — making timing your move thoughtfully quite worthwhile.
~
HOUSING MARKET
Rental prices in Lisbon and the Algarve remain elevated by local standards, with central Lisbon one-bedrooms averaging €1,400–€1,800/month and Algarve coastal towns commanding premiums in peak season. Buying property, however, continues to offer relatively stronger long-term value compared to renting, particularly outside the major tourist corridors.
